This Month I Learned Jekyll

September 2015

This month began with a friend's birthday. As a present, I gave her a year's worth of domain ($13.17 at Hover.com [referral link]). But then I realized that I wanted to write her the card on a website parked at that domain!

To be fiscally efficient, since the physical card I bought for her cost more than the domain, I searched for a free, yet premium hosting option. Free meant I did not need to pay money. Premium, in this context, meant permanently (no trials) void of ads or watermarks.

To this end, I tried GitHub Pages and found it to be just what I needed. In fact, this is how I'm hosting this page right now! The github repo that directly pipes to this website can be found here: https://github.com/jeffhou/jeffhou.github.io. Please feel free to comment on this or even submit pull requests ;).

Fascinated by the technology that seemed so simple, yet so foreign, I found my way to https://18f.gsa.gov/, the government organization that open sources the solutions they build. GitHub Repo: https://github.com/18F. There, I tried to tackle one of their easier open bugs/issues. Even though I did not end up solving the problem, I did make a comment! It was the first time I moved out of my comfort zone in terms of participating in an open source repo outside of class.

Working on the bug helped me understand more about Jekyll since I had to deploy my own Jekyll instance to test solutions to the bug. Here I used https://c9.io/ as my workspace. It's free and is an awesome online platform to develop webapps, but it just got acquired by Amazon and it asks for a credit card even for its free account. I won't delve into too much detail here, but here's the Hacker News discussion: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=12092983.

Now, I'm building and deploying my entire personal website using GitHub Pages, Jekyll and Cloud9! I'm really looking forward to working on this website more as I learn more and clean up past projects to share with the world. Please let me know if you have any questions! Just create an issue on GitHub and let's chat. :)
